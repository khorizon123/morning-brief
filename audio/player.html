U.S.-Iran War Escalates as American Forces Strike Deeper Inside Iran

American forces escalated their campaign overnight, hitting infrastructure deep inside Iran — including six bridges at a southern port, a railway, and an airport — according to Iranian state media. The strikes represent a significant expansion in the range of targets, as Trump seeks to maximize economic pressure on Tehran. The conflict has now shuttered most traffic through the Strait of Hormuz, a chokepoint through which roughly 20% of the world's oil normally flows, pushing energy prices sharply higher.

The U.S. and Iran have been exchanging military strikes for over a week, stemming from a clash over control of the Strait of Hormuz — a narrow waterway between Iran and the Arabian Peninsula that is the world's most critical oil transit route. The conflict began after renewed tensions and has already disrupted global energy markets.

Andy Burnham Becomes UK Labour Leader, Set to Be Prime Minister Monday

Andy Burnham, the former mayor of Greater Manchester, was confirmed today as leader of Britain's governing Labour Party, setting him up to become prime minister on Monday when he travels to Buckingham Palace to be formally sworn in. Burnham won with near-unanimous MP and union support — the only serious candidate — and faces scrutiny over vague policy positions on tax, immigration, and spending. He plans a rapid series of policy announcements in his early days, with cost of living, North Sea energy, and devolution set to feature.

Burnham replaces Sir Keir Starmer, who resigned after Labour's poll numbers deteriorated. In the UK, when a governing party changes its leader, the new leader typically becomes prime minister without a general election, heading to the palace for a ceremonial appointment by the monarch.

Diesel Prices Surge 20% as Iran War Chokes Strait of Hormuz Supplies

U.S. diesel futures have surged about 20% since the start of last week, with the national average hitting $5.01 per gallon and analysts warning of another 20–25 cents of increases to come. Domestic stockpiles are approaching the lowest levels since 2003, down 10% since late February, as the Iran war disrupts Strait of Hormuz traffic and Russia's export ban removes another major supply source. Long-haul semi-trucks alone burn over 100 million gallons of diesel a day in the U.S., meaning higher prices ripple immediately through the cost of nearly everything.

Diesel is the fuel of the freight economy — it powers trucks, trains, and ships that move goods across the country. A sustained spike in diesel prices acts as an indirect tax on consumers, raising the cost of food, building materials, and manufactured goods.

Astronomers Identify First Nearby Planet With an Atmosphere That Could Support Life

Astronomers have identified what they describe as the first potentially habitable nearby planet with a confirmed atmosphere — a significant milestone in the search for life beyond Earth. The planet is being described as a lead contender in the broader search for biosignatures, though researchers have not claimed evidence of life itself.

Detecting an atmosphere on a planet outside our solar system is extremely difficult and requires analyzing starlight passing through the planet's air. Most exoplanet atmospheres detected so far have been on gas giants; finding one on a potentially rocky, habitable-zone world is rare.

Moonshot AI's Kimi K3 Pulls Within Striking Distance of GPT-5.6 Sol and Claude Fable 5

Chinese lab Moonshot AI released Kimi K3, an open-weights model that lands at 57 on Artificial Analysis's Intelligence Index — behind only Claude Fable 5 (60) and OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol (59). K3 beats both frontier models on benchmarks for web research, spreadsheet work, frontend design, and long coding, while pricing at the same $3/$15 per million tokens as Claude 5 Sonnet. Moonshot says it will publish the model weights publicly by July 27. In a striking demo, the model ran autonomously for 48 hours to design and verify a chip that runs a miniature version of itself, hitting 8,700 tokens per second in simulation. Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei had recently estimated China was "6–12 months behind" U.S. frontier labs; this release suggests that gap may now be measured in a single model cycle.

Moonshot AI

Moonshot AI is a Beijing-based AI lab founded in 2023 that has rapidly built one of China's most capable large language models under the brand name "Kimi." Today's release of Kimi K3 — an open-weights model that benchmarks within two points of the world's best frontier models and will be freely downloadable — positions Moonshot as the clearest evidence yet that the gap between Chinese and Western AI labs is closing faster than U.S. executives predicted. The model features a 1-million-token context window (meaning it can process extremely long documents in a single pass), beats both Claude Fable 5 and GPT-5.6 Sol on several practical tasks, and is priced identically to mid-tier U.S. competitors. The surprising fact: in a demonstration, K3 ran autonomously for 48 hours to design and verify a miniature computer chip that runs a compressed version of itself — then simulated it at 8,700 tokens per second.
