Major League Soccer

Founded in 1996 as a condition of the U.S. hosting the 1994 World Cup, MLS now fields 30 teams (three in Canada) with a combined valuation around $23 billion, and counts hedge-fund billionaire David Tepper, Home Depot co-founder Arthur Blank, and Kraft Group CEO Robert Kraft among its owners. The league is riding record U.S. viewership from this summer's World Cup — won by the David Beckham rule that once brought in Beckham himself and later Lionel Messi — to try to close the gap with Europe's top leagues. It recently shifted its calendar to align with the European season and inked a deal with prediction-market platform Polymarket, complete with new guardrails against insider trading. Commissioner Don Garber, who has run the league since 1999, insists he'll never rule out promotion and relegation — but argues the closed-league model is what lets owners sink a billion dollars into a team and stadium with confidence. Surprising fact: KKR and MLS struck a private-equity deal worth up to $200 million just for the league's minor-league development tier, MLS Next Pro — a sign of how much capital is chasing even the feeder system of American soccer.
