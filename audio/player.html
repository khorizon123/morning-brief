World

US-Iran War Hits Ninth Night as Oil Tops $90 and Casualties Mount

American forces struck Iran for a ninth consecutive night after Iranian attacks killed three U.S. troops since Friday — including one soldier killed disposing of an Iranian drone in northern Iraq and two in a strike on a Jordan air base. Iran also struck Saudi Arabia and Kuwait and vowed not to let a "single drop" of oil pass through the Strait of Hormuz, while Brent crude climbed above $90 a barrel for the first time since June 11. Analysts warn the conflict is spiraling toward a wider war neither side can easily contain, and Iran's rial has fallen to a record low.

The U.S. and Iran have been trading strikes since a ceasefire broke down last month, with fighting spreading from direct exchanges into attacks on shipping and regional U.S. allies. The Strait of Hormuz and nearby Bab al-Mandeb chokepoint carry a huge share of the world's oil, so disruption there ripples through global energy prices.

Andy Burnham Becomes UK Prime Minister, Names Cabinet

Andy Burnham was sworn in as Britain's new prime minister today, taking over from Sir Keir Starmer after being confirmed as Labour leader last week. He's expected to name Shabana Mahmood as chancellor and unveil a blitz of policy announcements on devolution, social care and the water industry, alongside plans for a more centralized Downing Street less reliant on the Treasury.

Burnham, the former Greater Manchester mayor nicknamed "King of the North," takes office without having won a general election, having secured the Labour leadership unopposed after Starmer's resignation. He inherits a struggling economy, elevated inflation partly driven by the Iran-war oil shock, and a nervous gilt market.

Third US Soldier Dies as Iran War Casualties Mount

A third American service member died over the weekend during a controlled detonation of an Iranian drone in northern Iraq, following the deaths of two soldiers in a Friday attack on a Jordan air base. Iran has also struck Saudi Arabia for the first time in months and hit Kuwait and Bahrain, all countries hosting U.S. forces, in what officials describe as a widening regional escalation.

These are the first confirmed American combat deaths since a ceasefire between Washington and Tehran collapsed, marking a serious escalation after months of exchanges that had largely spared U.S. personnel directly.

Saudi-Houthi Truce Fraying, Raising Threat to Red Sea Oil Route

Growing hostilities between Saudi Arabia and Yemen's Houthi militia threaten a Red Sea pipeline route the kingdom has relied on to keep oil exports flowing while the Strait of Hormuz remains blockaded. Last week was one of the deadliest for commercial shipping since the U.S.-Iran war began, with at least three seafarers killed and over a dozen injured. Officials worry that regional dynamics could push the Houthis back toward attacking ships near Yemen's coast.

A 2022 truce had mostly kept Houthi attacks on Red Sea shipping in check after years of disruption; its breakdown would open a second maritime chokepoint to the same instability already hitting Hormuz.

Anthropic Ends Fable 5's Access Chaos, Halving Usage Caps

Anthropic finally settled weeks of shifting deadlines over Claude Fable 5 access, announcing the model stays permanently within Max and Team Premium plans but at half the previous usage caps; lower-tier subscribers get a one-time $100 credit before moving to pay-per-use. Anthropic called demand "challenging to predict" and said it's investing in more compute. The move follows competitive pressure from Moonshot's Kimi K3 and OpenAI's expanding GPT-5.6 Sol limits — and drew mocking jabs from OpenAI CEO Sam Altman on X about Anthropic "treating users with contempt."

OpenAI CFO Proposes 'Useful Intelligence Per Dollar' as New AI Budget Metric

OpenAI CFO Sarah Friar published a scorecard for grading corporate AI spending, arguing companies should measure "useful intelligence per dollar" rather than raw per-token pricing. Using the DeepSWE coding benchmark, OpenAI showed its Sol model scoring 2.8 points above Anthropic's Fable 5 with a 36.2% lower estimated API bill. The framework arrives as companies grow anxious about ballooning AI budgets — though critics note grading models by finished work rather than price conveniently favors expensive frontier labs like OpenAI itself.

Company

Anthropic

Anthropic, the AI lab behind the Claude model family, is having a chaotic but pivotal stretch: it just ended weeks of on-again, off-again plans to pull Claude Fable 5 from subscription plans, settling on halved usage caps rather than an outright cutoff after admitting demand was "challenging to predict." At the same time, the company is pursuing a fall IPO with Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley and JPMorgan lining up investor meetings, is in early talks to rent up to $10 billion of Meta's data-center capacity, and recently launched Ode, an enterprise AI services arm backed by Blackstone and Hellman & Friedman. It's also facing pointed public jabs from OpenAI's Sam Altman, who mocked its access squeeze on X as treating users "with contempt." Surprising fact: despite being one of the most richly funded AI labs in the world, Anthropic's biggest recent problem hasn't been model quality — it's been having enough spare computing capacity to serve its own paying customers.'
