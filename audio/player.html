US-Iran War Enters Second Week as American Strikes Push Deeper Into Iran

American forces carried out a seventh consecutive night of strikes on Iran, hitting surveillance sites, military logistics infrastructure and underground weapons storage, while also destroying a port surveillance tower US Central Command said was being used to track commercial vessels in the Strait of Hormuz. Iran retaliated by firing on Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman and Qatar, with Kuwait reporting hits on power and water plants that ignited fires and damaged generators. Oil prices have jumped to a one-month high and diesel remains elevated as traffic through the Strait nears a halt.

The US and Iran have been trading strikes for over a week after a ceasefire collapsed. The Strait of Hormuz is one of the world's most important oil chokepoints, and disruption there ripples through global energy prices.

Ukraine Protests Enter Second Day Over Ousted Drone-Warfare Chief

Thousands of Ukrainians protested for a second consecutive day against President Zelensky's dismissal of popular defense minister Mykhailo Fedorov, the 35-year-old architect of the country's drone program. Zelensky said he was forced to "choose between sides" and wanted unity, while reporting suggests generals resisted Fedorov's modernization push. Separately, new tracking data shows Ukrainian drone strikes on Crimea have surged, with more than half of all strikes since 2022 occurring in just the past year, straining Russian supply lines.

Fedorov built Ukraine's asymmetric drone warfare capability from scratch, redirecting funds from salaries to hardware. His removal has exposed a rift between reformers and the traditional military command over how to fight Russia.

Andy Burnham Set to Become UK Prime Minister Monday, Names Chancellor Pick

Andy Burnham, the former Greater Manchester mayor known as "king of the north," will be asked by King Charles to form a government Monday after being confirmed as Labour leader. Home Secretary Shabana Mahmood, an immigration hawk from the party's right, is set to become chancellor, reassuring businesses wary of a leftward lurch. Burnham is expected to move quickly on the cost of living, North Sea energy, social care and devolution.

Burnham takes over from Sir Keir Starmer, whose government lost the confidence of Labour's base despite a landslide 2024 win. Britain's economy grew just 0.1% in May, leaving little room for the bold promises Burnham campaigned on.

AI & Tech

Moonshot's Kimi K3 Rattles Markets, Narrows US-China AI Gap

Chinese startup Moonshot released Kimi K3, a 2.8-trillion-parameter open-weights model with a 1-million-token context window, whose benchmark results reportedly rival or exceed OpenAI's and Anthropic's frontier models in coding and agent tasks. Open weights are scheduled for release July 27. The UK's AI Security Institute — the world's leading AI-testing body — says the gap between Chinese and US developers has shrunk to as little as four months, raising fears that cheap, freely available models could soon be weaponized by hackers. The release helped trigger a global selloff in AI and chip stocks, with some comparing it to last year's DeepSeek shock.

Poolhouse

Poolhouse is a two-year-old British startup founded by twin entrepreneurs Steve and Dave Jolliffe — the same duo behind Topgolf and PuttShack — aiming to do for pool halls what Topgolf did for driving ranges. Its flagship venue, a two-story, 21,000-square-foot hall in central London, pairs upscale hospitality (a Gordon Ramsay-trained chef, Vegas-style bars) with its own tech platform, BillyQ, built by the same team behind Hawk-Eye ball-tracking used in pro tennis and baseball. BillyQ uses cameras, AI and projectors to track shots, assign handicaps and run arcade-style games on the table. Poolhouse just raised $55 million led by Bluestone Equity Partners at a valuation north of $100 million, and its real ambition is licensing BillyQ to other operators — hotels, cinemas, cruise ships — rather than just running venues itself. Surprising fact: the projectors that power BillyQ are currently bulky and expensive, but the company expects to eventually shrink the cost to roughly that of a Peloton bike.
