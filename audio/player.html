U.S.-Iran Ceasefire Collapses as Strait of Hormuz Battle Intensifies

The U.S. carried out a fifth round of strikes on Iran — described as involving jet fighters, drones and warships against roughly 140 targets — after Iran's Revolutionary Guard fired on commercial shipping in the Strait of Hormuz. Iran retaliated by hitting targets in Bahrain, Kuwait, Jordan and Oman, and declared the strait closed. Only 13 ships transited the waterway on Friday, down from 51 on Tuesday, and Brent crude climbed above $79 a barrel. Iran's Foreign Ministry said the June peace framework had "undoubtedly entered a crisis phase."

The Strait of Hormuz is the narrow chokepoint between Iran and Oman through which roughly 20% of the world's oil supply passes. The U.S. and Iran reached a preliminary ceasefire in June but left control of the strait ambiguous — Iran has exploited that gap to assert dominance over which vessels may pass, triggering escalating rounds of tit-for-tat military strikes.

Marco Rubio Is Venezuela's De Facto Viceroy — and Post-Quake Rage Is Testing That Grip

An investigation by the New York Times reveals that Secretary of State Marco Rubio exercises sweeping, day-to-day control over Venezuela since U.S. commandos captured Nicolás Maduro in January — controlling the country's export revenues, approving ministerial appointments, and texting Venezuelan acting president Delcy Rodríguez even during official U.S. diplomatic visits. Meanwhile, devastating earthquakes in June collapsed state-built public housing, killing at least 3,500 people and triggering rare, open public fury at the government — and at the U.S. — in a country still controlled by a vast repressive apparatus.

The Trump administration seized Maduro earlier this year in what it framed as a law enforcement operation. The U.S. Treasury now effectively controls Venezuela's oil revenues, disbursing funds to Caracas in exchange for political compliance. The collapsed housing, built under the late Hugo Chávez's social programs, had long been flagged as structurally unsafe.

Senator Lindsey Graham Dies at 71 — A Tear in His Aorta, His Office Says

Republican Senator Lindsey Graham of South Carolina died suddenly over the weekend; a preliminary medical examiner's report cited a tear in his aorta. Graham had evolved from a scathing early critic of Donald Trump to one of his most loyal surrogates, championing U.S. strikes on Iran and support for Israel and Ukraine. His death, along with Mitch McConnell's ongoing hospitalization following a fall, narrows the Republican Senate majority and complicates the confirmation of several pending cabinet nominees.

Graham's death leaves a meaningful gap in foreign policy hawkishness on Capitol Hill. South Carolina will almost certainly remain in Republican hands, but his seat will be vacant in the near term, and his loss removes an influential voice in Trump's inner circle on Iran, Israel and Ukraine.

Zelensky Dismisses Ukraine's Prime Minister in Cabinet Overhaul

President Volodymyr Zelensky announced a sweeping cabinet reshuffle, including the dismissal of Prime Minister Yulia Svyrydenko, less than a year after the previous shake-up. Zelensky said the changes were needed to execute a new political strategy, with priorities including managing relations with Washington and the EU and securing continued weapons supplies. Sergy Koretsky, head of Ukraine's largest state-owned oil-and-gas firm, is reportedly a leading candidate for the prime ministerial role.

This is Ukraine's third major government shake-up since Russia's full-scale invasion in February 2022. Zelensky has increasingly treated government reshuffles as a tool for projecting decisiveness to Western allies, whose continued support — particularly from the U.S. — remains uncertain.

Vinod Khosla Buys Seattle Seahawks for Record $9.6 Billion

A group led by venture capitalist Vinod Khosla has agreed to purchase the Seattle Seahawks for a reported $9.6 billion — a record price for an NFL franchise, topping the $6.05 billion Josh Harris paid for the Washington Commanders in 2023. The Seahawks had been held by the estate of Microsoft co-founder Paul Allen since his 2018 death. Khosla, who Forbes recently ranked No. 1 on its Midas List of top venture investors (citing early bets on OpenAI, DoorDash and Instacart), beat out rival bids including one from Indian tycoon Aditya Mittal. His wife Neeru Khosla will become the controlling owner upon NFL approval; Vinod will be required to divest a 3.1% stake he holds in the San Francisco 49ers.

Company

Helsing

Helsing is a European defense AI company, headquartered in Munich, that builds software to help militaries process sensor data, coordinate unmanned systems and make faster battlefield decisions — effectively serving as an AI brain for modern defense platforms. It just raised $1.8 billion at an $18 billion valuation from Goldman Sachs Alternatives, JPMorgan Chase and Lightspeed Venture Partners, making it one of the most valuable defense tech startups in Europe. The round coincides with plans to open its first U.S. attack drone manufacturing facility in West Virginia — a significant geographic expansion that signals ambitions far beyond its European home market. What makes Helsing unusual is its explicit focus on 'sovereignty': it only works with NATO-aligned governments and structures its contracts to ensure client nations retain control over the AI systems deployed in their militaries. Surprisingly, the company was founded in 2021 — meaning it reached an $18 billion valuation in just five years, faster than almost any defense company in history.
